What is the technology?

Virtual reality is a 3D computer simulated environment that completely immerses the user through a headset. It allows the user to interact with virtual surroundings, often using handheld controllers. The headset covers your eyes to create a 360-degree, 3D experience. The controllers are held in your hand to manipulate objects in the virtual world. Virtual reality is a popular technology used today in gaming, education, training, and healthcare.

Real-world applications

Virtual reality is used across many industries for immersive training, simulation, entertainment and therapy. VR enables cost-effective, realistic, and safe training, such as simulations for pilots and hazardous scenarios for employees. It is widely used in medical education for surgical training. VR is also heavily used in physical rehabilitation and cognitive Behavioral Therapy. It also assists in treating Alzheimer patients. Aside from medical and training relations, VR is used in engineering, manufacturing and real estate. People use virtual reality to help visualize and test 3D prototypes of machines or vehicles, allowing them to have a good idea of what it looks like before physical production. Lastly, Virtual reality is most popular for gaming and entertainment. People use VR to play simulations and feel like they’re in the video game world. Aside from gaming, VR is used for 3D cinema, immersive journalism, and social experiences.

Social impacts

Virtual reality offers transformative benefits through its enhanced entertainment and immersive training, however, it carries an equal amount of risks such as motion sickness, addiction, and social isolation. VR allows for realistic, safe and cost-effective training in fields such as medicine, aviation and manufacturing, allowing trainees to be able to make mistakes without any real-world consequences. Additionally, VR provides immersive learning experiences, such as virtual field trips to historical sites and different parts of the world. Virtual Reality also offers various activities for people to do, and fitness focused VR games motivate participants to exercise and increase physical activity. On the contrary, virtual reality can cause ‘cybersickness,’ meaning that users feel nauseous, dizziness, vertigo, headaches and more. The immersive world and 3D images so close to your eyes can cause motion sickness and more. Long-term use of VR can also cause eye-strain and fatigue. Furthermore, VR is often used as a way to escape reality, and the highly immersive environments can lead to excessive use, and ultimately addiction. VR usage can cause people to spend hours on it without realizing, and can reduce face-to-face human interaction, overall causing social isolation. These positive and negative impacts are the social effects of virtual reality. The immersive virtual world can cause many things to happen, but when used correctly, can be very beneficial.
