What is the technology?

Virtual reality is a 3D computer simulated environment that completely immerses the user through a headset. It allows the user to interact with virtual surroundings, often using handheld controllers. The headset covers your eyes to create a 360-degree, 3D experience. The controllers are held in your hand to manipulate objects in the virtual world. Virtual reality is a popular technology used today in gaming, education, training, and healthcare.

Real-world applications

Virtual reality is used across many industries for immersive training, simulation, entertainment and therapy. VR enables cost-effective, realistic, and safe training, such as simulations for pilots and hazardous scenarios for employees. It is widely used in medical education for surgical training. VR is also heavily used in physical rehabilitation and cognitive Behavioral Therapy. It also assists in treating Alzheimer patients. Aside from medical and training relations, VR is used in engineering, manufacturing and real estate. People use virtual reality to help visualize and test 3D prototypes of machines or vehicles, allowing them to have a good idea of what it looks like before physical production. Lastly, Virtual reality is most popular for gaming and entertainment. People use VR to play simulations and feel like they’re in the video game world. Aside from gaming, VR is used for 3D cinema, immersive journalism, and social experiences.
